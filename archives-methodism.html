Archives of Ohio United Methodism

The Collections


The collection functions as an archival and historical repository and research center. Items which represent the heritage of The United Methodist Church in Ohio from its inception up to and including the present day will be selected. Primary emphasis is on the East and West Ohio Conferences with other materials maintained from antecedent conferences. The Archives Center has several collections:

  • Books about the history of United Methodism in general
  • Manuscripts and conference proceedings relevant to the history of Ohio United Methodism
  • Artifacts, photos, and other museum objects of good quality that depict the history of United Methodism in Ohio
  • Publications of The United Methodist Church
  • Local church history files
  • Records of closed and abandoned churches
  • Dead Ministers file (index to clergy who have died - service records & locations of memoirs, photos, etc.)
  • Newspapers, journals, and periodicals including Western Christian Advocate, Ladies' Repository, Der Christliche Apologete, Haus und Herd, Der Christliche Botschafter, Methodist History
  • Personal papers and diaries, including J.B. Finley letters, Bishop Thomas A. Morris' papers, and the Roy Leedy Evangelical collection

Research in the Archives


The Archives of Ohio United Methodism is a rich resource for researching church history, clergy members, and genealogy. The Archives does offer research services to those interested in any of these areas. You can submit a request to the archivist with the details of your research needs.

About the Archives


The Archives of Ohio United Methodism is a collection of books, manuscripts, pamphlets, periodicals, artifacts, and other materials organized to encourage the study of the history of United Methodism in Ohio. The Archives is housed at Beeghly Library, Ohio Wesleyan University and is jointly sponsored and supported by the University and the East and West Ohio Conferences of The United Methodist Church.
